Fyodor Dostoevsky
Fyodor Mikhaylovich Dostoevsky


crime and punishment, dostoevsky








Fyodor Dostoevsky, the renowed Russian author, became a phenomenon in the world
of literature during the mid-19th century. His writing style that is greatly rich with
psychological depth, philosophical themes, and vivid potrayals of human emotions has
regarded him as one of the greatest novelist in history.

While reading one of his greatest work, Crime and Punishment, my first thought was
that the novel is going to discuss about the crime and the punishment equally. Since
the book was divided into 6 parts, I thought the first half of the book is about the crime
and the second half is about the punishment. But after finishing the novel I realised
the punishment in the novel is not the punishment that I was expecting at all.

Dostoevsky explores the effects of guilt and the psychological torment it inflicts on
the main character, Raskolnikov. He delves into Raskolnikov's inner turmoil,
his conflicting beliefs, and his descent into madness. Dostoevsky's portrayal of guilt
is haunting, capturing the psychological weight and torment that Raskolnikov experiences
as he confronts his own conscience and seeks redemption.

Crime and Punishment was the first Dostoevsky's novel that I read and soon after
I finished it I quickly understood why Dostoevsky was such a phenomenon author.
His view in human psychology and morality is very fascinating and the way he potrays
it in his writing creates a powerful impact to the readers.

Dostoevsky is one of my favorite author and I wish to read more of his works.
